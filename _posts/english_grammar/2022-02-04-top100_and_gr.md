## parts of speech

- **noun**(명사) : a word (other than a pronoun) used to identify any of a class of people, places, or things (common noun), or to name a particular one of these (proper noun)
- **pronoun**(대명사) : a word that can function by itself as a noun phrase and that refers either to the participants in the discourse or to someone or something mentioned elsewhere in the discourse
- **verb**(동사) : a word used to describe an action, state, or occurrence, and forming the main part of the predicate of a sentence
- **adjective**(형용사) : a word or phrase naming an attribute, added to or grammatically related to a noun to modify or describe it
- **adverb**(부사) : a word or phrase that modifies or qualifies an adjective, verb, or other adverb or a word group, expressing a relation of place, time, circumstance, manner, cause, degree, etc.
- **preposition**(전치사) : a word governing, and usually preceding, a noun or pronoun and expressing a relation to another word or element in the clause
- **conjunction**(접속사) : a word used to connect clauses or sentences or to coordinate words in the same clause
- **interjection**(감탄사) : an abrupt remark, made especially as an aside or interruption
- **phrase**(구) : a small group of words standing together as a conceptual unit, typically forming a component of a clause
- **clause**(절) : a unit of grammatical organization next below the sentence in rank and in traditional grammar said to consist of a subject and predicate
